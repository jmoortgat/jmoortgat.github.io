## 3. ABOUT / SUMMARY  (~2,000 chars — well within 2,600 limit)
## ══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

I'm a Full Professor in the School of Earth Sciences at The Ohio State University and the founding Director of the BuckAI Observatory — OSU's new interdisciplinary center for AI-driven Earth observation, launched in August 2025 with $1M in institutional seed funding.

My research spans two directions. For over 15 years I have been developing higher-order finite element methods for multiphase compositional flow in fractured porous media, with applications to CO₂ sequestration, shale gas, and natural hydrogen reservoirs. My group holds 4 patent families with 10 fully issued patents (US, Japan, Saudi Arabia, South Africa) on subsurface hydrogen exploration and production.

More recently — beginning during my 2020–2021 sabbatical — I pivoted toward deep learning applied to multi-modal satellite imagery. We now build AI tools to map rivers, coastlines, and forests from space, prospect for natural hydrogen reservoirs using SAM and Sentinel-2, and track deforestation and agroforestry in Sub-Saharan Africa.

Background: PhD in astrophysics (Radboud University, 2006), MSc in Physics (under Nobel Laureate Gerard 't Hooft, Utrecht) and Astronomy (Utrecht), postdoc at University of Rochester and RERI (Palo Alto, with Abbas Firoozabadi). Joined OSU in 2013; Full Professor since 2024.

h-index: 31 · i10-index: 47 · 66 peer-reviewed publications · SPE Ferguson Medal 2014

📊 Google Scholar: https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=0onLASoAAAAJ
